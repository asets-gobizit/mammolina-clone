The Montessori approach is a holistic experience<\/strong> that thrives when both family and school walk the same path.<\/p>

Choosing a school is a significant decision.<\/strong> In Montessori, we aren't just talking about a physical space, but a specific way of understanding childhood and learning.<\/span><\/p>

The topics below are designed to give you a first look at our philosophy<\/strong> and help you better understand what you will see and experience when you step into our classrooms.<\/span><\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

We offer a \"prepared environment\" where children develop inner discipline, responsibility, and a genuine desire to learn. Our space is calm, respectful, and nurturing, specifically designed to support their daily growth.<\/span><\/p>

We believe in self-construction: when the right conditions are met, a child\u2019s potential unfolds from within. This is why we view <\/span>education as \"preparation for life,\"<\/b>not just academic instruction.<\/span><\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/details>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t

THE MONTESSORI FOUNDATIONS <\/div><\/span>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t
\n\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t

    \n \t
  • Developmental Planes:<\/b> We understand that children move through distinct stages (0\u20136 and 6\u201312), each with specific psychological needs. Our environment and curriculum are built to meet these unique characteristics.<\/span>\n\n<\/span><\/li>
    \n \t
  • The Absorbent and Reasoning Minds:<\/b> In the early years, learning is sensory, concrete, and based on repetition. In the Primary years, reason and imagination become the primary engines of knowledge.<\/span>\n\n<\/span><\/li>
    \n \t
  • Individualized Learning:<\/b> Every child progresses at their own pace within a structured and carefully prepared framework.<\/span>\n\n<\/span><\/li>
    \n \t
  • Intrinsic Motivation:<\/b> We do not use external rewards or punishments. Instead, we foster concentration, responsibility, and a natural pride in a job well done.<\/span>\n\n<\/span><\/li>
    \n \t
  • Community and Social Life:<\/b> Our mixed-age classrooms (3\u20136 and 6\u201312) function as a \"mini-society\" where children learn to cooperate, lead, ask for help, and resolve conflicts. Grace and Courtesy are explicit parts of our curriculum, cultivating empathy and respect.<\/span><\/li>\n \t<\/li>
    \n \t
  • A Multilingual Environment:<\/b> Our project is international and multilingual. English, French, and Spanish are woven into daily school life. Language support is tailored to each child's developmental stage, ensuring a steady and natural progression in all three languages.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/details>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t

      Montessori is not a \"fast-track\" academic or competitive model. We do not prioritize constant comparisons or immediate, superficial results.<\/span><\/p>

      If you are looking for a structured environment that respects natural development and focuses on autonomy, responsibility, and community life, this visit will be incredibly valuable to you.<\/span><\/p>
